Biography

I am a biostatistician at Children's Mercy Research Institute in Kansas City, Missouri. My research interests include seamless oncology clinical trial methodology and statistical methods for spatial protein imaging data.

Research

  • Trivariate co-localization: Developing new statistical estimators for quantifying three cell population co-localization in spatial protein imaging data. Statistics formulated using a triangle's longest edge poorly distinguish between tight and dispersed point process triples. Thus, we propose a new area-based Horvitz-Thompson estimator using Monte Carlo sampling to reduce enumeration time by >100,000x. This estimator also preserves key statistical properties, such as unbiasedness and consistency for the fully enumerated statistics.
